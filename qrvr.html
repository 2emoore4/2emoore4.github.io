mobile head tracking (WIP)
september 2015

for virtual reality applications, developers must wield expensive equipment in order to do any head tracking. some people use mocap systems, which are basically infrared cameras. the big products like Oculus and Vive are using infrared lasers and sensors. these solutions are not only potentially expensive, but they also require external equipment.

before Valve partnered with HTC for the Vive, they used a headset with a camera mounted on it. the camera would point at codes that were printed and taped onto the wall. since the camera could see the positions of the codes, it could locate itself within the room.

I've started to build a web app that will track qr codes. when a phone is mounted into a cardboard headset, the user can sit in front of a computer screen, or a wall with paper codes, and the headset will be able to determine its own position based on the position of the codes. not only is this cheap and easy, but it also eliminates the need to send head positions over a network.

so far I've implemented the qr code tracker, which finds the code's three alignment tags. I'll still need to come up with a stereo 3d visualization to demonstrate the head tracking.
