in the spring of 2014, my professor Ken Perlin co-taught a course at the MIT Media Lab called Shared Tangible Augmented Reality. the class focused on creating shared virtual spaces where participants could communicate and interact with each other.

for our final project, we were encouraged to explore person-to-person communication. our original idea was to create some kind of avatar that was passively controlled by human input like mobile device accelerometers, heart rate trackers, microphones, etc. however at some point we moved away from putting sensors on a body to putting sensors on a stationary device. this device ended up being a decorative fishbowl that people would keep in their home. this fishbowl would represent another person, and it would be a portal for communicating with that person.
